# Labour Migrants (Arbeidsmigranten) — Problem Research

Research via web-search agent, April 2026.

---

## Scale

- **600,000–800,000** foreign workers in low-skilled NL jobs; 40,000–50,000 added per year
- **>50%** of ~407,000 temp-agency workers born abroad
- Top nationalities: Poland (~82,500 temp workers), Romania + Ukraine + Bulgaria (~66,700 combined)
- Total EU mobility workers including unregistered: **~900,000–1 million**
- Amsterdam region: Schiphol logistics corridor, Westpoort distribution, Aalsmeer flower industry — described as "entirely dependent" on Eastern European labour

**Key sectors:** logistics/distribution, greenhouse horticulture, construction, meat processing, cleaning, agriculture

---

## Most Common Violations

### 1. Wage below minimum (WML-fraude)
- **~30–40% of NLA inspections** in high-risk sectors find violations
- Mechanism: housing/transport deductions from gross wage push net pay below statutory minimum
- SNCU ran 347 CAO investigations in 2023

### 2. Koppeling wonen-werk (housing tied to employment)
- Workers lose housing the moment job ends — creates total dependency, suppresses complaints
- **Wet goed verhuurderschap (July 2023)** explicitly prohibits this linkage
- Enforcement: municipalities responsible but capacity lacking — prohibition exists, practice continues

### 3. Housing quality
- SNF-certified accommodation is standard, but uncertified (overcrowded, 8–12 per house) is widespread
- Roemer Commission (2020): documented systemic substandard housing as a national finding
- Fatalities in fires at migrant worker accommodation (Moerdijk 2020)

### 4. Vakantiegeld (8% holiday pay) not paid
- Routinely not paid or hidden in hourly rate without transparency
- Among most common CAO breaches in temp-agency sector (SNCU data)

### 5. Ziekengeld (sick pay) denied
- Workers dismissed when sick, employer claims "assignment ends"
- Reality: Ziektewet applies to most flex workers — workers don't know this

### 6. Retaliatory dismissal
- Workers fear contacting FNV or ISZW will cost them their job AND housing simultaneously
- Retaliatory dismissal is illegal but workers don't know this

## What Workers Don't Know — Most Impactful Rights Gaps

| Right | Why critical |
|---|---|
| Housing cannot legally be tied to employment | Removes the #1 mechanism suppressing complaints |
| Net pay after all deductions must meet minimum wage | Most common wage theft mechanism exploits this ignorance |
| Ziekengeld applies to most flex workers | Workers are routinely dismissed illegally when sick |
| Vakantiegeld is owed on top of wage (8%) | Routinely stolen; workers think it's optional |
| 6-week bezwaar window after wrongful dismissal | Deadline missed = rights lost permanently |
| Retaliatory dismissal is illegal and contestable | Primary reason workers don't report anything |
| SNCU and ISZW complaint procedures | Enforcement bodies exist, workers can't find them |

**Roemer Commission recommendation (unimplemented):** Workers should be informed of their rights **in their native language on day 1 of employment**. Not consistently done.

---

## How Workers Actually Get Information

1. **Facebook groups** — "Polacy w Holandii" and Romanian/Bulgarian equivalents. Primary channel for practical info AND misinformation. Fraudulent recruitment also spreads here.
2. **WhatsApp networks** — employer-run groups (suppress complaints) and compatriot groups (trusted). Workers share pay slips, employer reviews.
3. **Word of mouth** — most trusted. A worker who resolved a problem becomes an informal advisor.
4. **Google in native language** — fragmentary results, often outdated, not NL-specific.
5. Formal channels (FNV, SNCU, ISZW) reached by **small minority** — primarily longer-term residents with higher language skills.

## Existing Support — and Why It Fails

| Organisation | What it does | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| FNV Migrants | Hotline, campaigns, some Polish/Romanian materials | Minimal digital tools; workers don't know FNV exists |
| SNCU | Enforces temp-agency CAOs, recovers back pay | Complaint-triggered only; workers don't know SNCU exists |
| ISZW / NLA | Government inspectorate, anonymous tip line | Low trust — fear of immigration consequences (even for EU citizens) |
| Auroned (Amsterdam) | Romanian migrant coaching, virtual helpdesk | Very small scale |
| Geldfit.nl | Financial guidance in Polish/Romanian/Bulgarian | Financial/debt scope only, no labour rights |

**No tool exists** that explains labour rights in Polish/Romanian/Bulgarian, identifies illegal deductions, or guides through SNCU/ISZW complaint procedure.

## Key Recent Legislation

**Wet goed verhuurderschap — July 2023**
- Prohibits koppeling wonen-werk (housing-employment linkage)
- Municipalities must enforce + run anonymous reporting points
- Enforcement variable and weak

**Wet toelating terbeschikkingstelling van arbeidskrachten (WTTA) — passed Nov 2025**
- Mandatory licensing for all temp agencies, payroll, secondment companies
- Requirements: Chamber of Commerce registration, Certificate of Good Conduct, €100K deposit, inspection compliance
- Hirers using unlicensed agencies face fines
- **Enforcement starts January 2028** — not yet in force

**Roemer Commission "Geen tweederangsburgers" (2020)**
- Landmark report; 50+ recommendations
- As of 2025: progress tracker shows "hardly any" progress on most recommendations
- Housing certification, registration, worker information remain underimplemented

## AI Tool — Highest Leverage Points

These 4 rights are: most commonly violated + most unknown + most unambiguously binary (ideal for AI):

1. **Koppeling wonen-werk is illegal** — employer cannot take your housing when firing you
2. **WML covers net pay after deductions** — housing/transport costs cannot push you below minimum wage
3. **Ziekengeld applies to flex workers** — being sent home sick is illegal in most cases
4. **6-week bezwaar window** — must be flagged immediately when dismissal happens

**Distribution channel:** Facebook groups and WhatsApp are where workers are. A tool that works via WhatsApp in Polish/Romanian = meets them where they are.

**FNV tie-in:** FNV Migrants gets a structured intake report instead of blank intake form. Doubles FNV advisor capacity per worker helped. Business model: FNV or NLA licenses the tool.

## Hard Statistics (Pitch-Ready)

| Metric | Number | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Labour migrants in NL | ~950,000 | CBS/Government 2023 |
| Share employed via temp agencies | ~50% | NLA/ABU |
| Companies inspected with violations | **69%** | NLA Nov 2024 |
| Companies partially shut down | **12%** | NLA Nov 2024 |
| Back pay recovered (sample) | €300K for 179 workers (~€1,676/worker) | NLA 2023 |
| Unlawful assets seized (criminal) | **€3.2M** | NLA 3yr program |
| Max legal housing deduction | **25% of minimum wage** (only if SNF-certified) | CAO/Law |
| Share of accidents vs workforce share | **22% of accidents, only 5–7% of workforce** | NLA |
| WTL mandatory effective date | **January 1, 2026** | Parliament April 2025 |
| Roemer recommendations (2020) | ~50 — most still unimplemented | Roemer Committee |

**Triple dependency:** Employer controls work + housing + transport simultaneously — losing job = losing home = losing transport in one blow. This is the structural mechanism that suppresses all reporting.

**Iceberg problem:** NLA explicitly acknowledges formal complaints are the "tip of the iceberg" — no published underreporting rate but systematically described as a small fraction of actual violations.

**Meat sector concentration:** 85% of temp workers at white-meat slaughterhouses are labour migrants — highest risk concentration of any sector.

**Westland case (Oct 2024):** NLA found 219 Ukrainian workers with illegal housing deductions (no SNF certification) + underpayment + failure to register with UWV — all simultaneously.
