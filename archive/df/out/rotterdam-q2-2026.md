# Heineken N.V. — Climate-Action Package Impact Report
## Q2 2026 — Voedselbank Rotterdam

### Executive Summary

In the second quarter of 2026, Heineken N.V. sponsored Voedselbank Rotterdam under a Climate-Action Package valued at €25,000. Applying the platform's locked attribution model, the sponsorship is associated with 600.0 tCO2e of avoided greenhouse-gas emissions and 323,389 kg of food rescued from waste. This corresponds to 7.77 per cent of Voedselbank Rotterdam's annual avoided-emissions baseline of 7,718.3 tCO2e for the relevant operating period.

The disclosure is structured for inclusion in Heineken N.V.'s ESRS E1 (climate change) sustainability statement under the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive. Avoided emissions are derived from a deterministic attribution model anchored in Voedselbank Rotterdam's published 2024 operational data and category-specific emission factors applied under a methodology aligned with the Global Foodbanking Network's FRAME framework. Supporting workings, sources and assumptions are reproduced verbatim in the Methodology Appendix.

Voedselbank Rotterdam is a Voedselbanken Nederland Regional Distribution Centre, supplying 33 satellite foodbanks across the Zuid-Holland region, and reaches 1,682 households weekly across that network.

### Methodology Overview

This report is methodology-aligned with the Global Foodbanking Network's FRAME framework (Food Recovery to Avoid Methane Emissions); it is not FRAME-certified. Avoided emissions are attributed to the sponsor on a pro-rata basis: the package's economic share of the foodbank's annual operations equals the sponsor's claimed share of avoided emissions, locked at €41.67 per tCO2e. Category-specific emission factors are sourced from FAO Food Wastage Footprint (2013), WRAP Courtauld Commitment 2030, RIVM Dutch dairy LCA, and Poore & Nemecek (2018). The disposal-route counterfactual reflects the Dutch national waste-treatment mix — predominantly incineration with energy recovery, with smaller fractions for composting, anaerobic digestion and animal feed — rather than the US-default landfill assumption used in some FRAME calculations. The NL counterfactual factor of 0.93 is applied to the weighted production-stage average to reflect the lower disposal-stage emissions of the Dutch route.

The attributed 600.0 tCO2e is derived from the package amount of €25,000 divided by the platform's locked package price of €41.67 per tCO2e. The corresponding 323,389 kg of food rescued represents the implied throughput at Voedselbank Rotterdam's category-weighted emission factor of 1.855 kg CO2e per kg food rescued. The 7.77 per cent attribution share is calculated as the ratio of attributed avoided emissions (600.0 tCO2e) to the foodbank's annual avoided-emissions baseline (7,718.3 tCO2e).

### Social Impact (ESRS S3)

The sponsorship contributes to outcomes for communities affected by food insecurity in the Rotterdam region. Voedselbank Rotterdam serves 1,682 households weekly and operates as a Voedselbanken Nederland Regional Distribution Centre supplying 33 satellite foodbanks across Zuid-Holland. Within Heineken N.V.'s broader affected-communities disclosure under ESRS S3, this sponsorship constitutes a measurable contribution to local food access and is consistent with the Just Transition principle that climate mitigation should not displace basic-needs provision for vulnerable populations. The foodbank publishes household-level coverage statistics; a unified persons-served figure is not reported by the foodbank for this period and is therefore omitted.

### Methodology Appendix

#### Calculation Trail

```
Sponsor: Heineken N.V.
Quarter: Q2 2026
Foodbank: Voedselbank Rotterdam (Zuid-Holland)
Sponsor amount: €25,000
Package economics: €25,000 per 600 tCO2e (€41.67 per tCO2e)

Step 1 — Bank operational throughput
  Annual food rescued: 4,160,000 kg
  Households served weekly: 1,682
  Source: Annual rescue cited directly in Voedselbank Rotterdam Jaarverslag 2024 as 'ongeveer 80.000 kilogram per week' across the Rotterdam regional network (incl. 33 satellite banks). Category mix derived from published parcel composition (13 of 25 units fresh F&V for 2-person households).

Step 2 — Category mix and weighted emission factor
  produce: 50% × 1.0 kg CO2e/kg = 0.500
  dry_goods: 18% × 2.0 kg CO2e/kg = 0.360
  dairy: 10% × 3.2 kg CO2e/kg = 0.320
  bakery: 10% × 1.5 kg CO2e/kg = 0.150
  meat: 5% × 8.5 kg CO2e/kg = 0.425
  prepared: 5% × 3.0 kg CO2e/kg = 0.150
  eggs: 2% × 4.5 kg CO2e/kg = 0.090
  Weighted factor before counterfactual: 1.995 kg CO2e/kg
  NL counterfactual factor: ×0.93
  Final weighted factor: 1.855 kg CO2e/kg food rescued

Step 3 — Bank-level annual avoided emissions
  4,160,000 kg × 1.855 kg CO2e/kg = 7,718.3 tCO2e/yr

Step 4 — Sponsor attribution (pro-rata by package economics)
  Attributed tCO2e = €25,000 ÷ €41.67/tCO2e = 600.0 tCO2e
  Attributed kg food = 600.0 tCO2e × 1000 ÷ 1.855 = 323,389 kg
  Attribution share = 600.0 ÷ 7718.3 = 7.77% of bank annual rescue
```

### Disclaimers and Limitations

- **Methodology alignment, not certification.** The calculation framework is aligned with the Global Foodbanking Network's FRAME methodology but has not been certified by GFN or any third-party verifier. References to FRAME indicate methodological alignment only.
- **Counterfactual assumptions.** The disposal-route counterfactual reflects the Dutch national waste-treatment mix at the time of publication. Where supermarket donation contracts specify alternative disposal routes (for example dedicated composting or anaerobic digestion), the avoided-emissions estimate may be conservative or, in less common cases, overstated.
- **Data freshness and review schedule.** Foodbank operational figures are sourced from the most recent published annual report (2024). The platform reviews and refreshes inputs on a quarterly basis; the next scheduled review is Q3 2026.
- **Attribution model.** Avoided emissions are attributed on a pro-rata basis tied to the package's locked economic ratio of €41.67 per tCO2e. The model assumes that sponsor funding is fungible across foodbank operations rather than directed to a specific food-rescue activity.
- **Independent verification status.** This report has not been subject to independent third-party assurance under ISAE 3000 or equivalent. Sponsors seeking limited or reasonable assurance for inclusion in their CSRD assurance scope should engage a qualified provider.
