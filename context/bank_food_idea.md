### Sharpened version

-   **Problem:** food banks are under-resourced and only reach a small share of eligible people, while their climate impact goes mostly unmeasured.
    
-   **Insight:** rescued food is both **social impact** and **climate impact**.
    
-   **Product:** a platform that helps food banks **track avoided food waste emissions** and turns that into reporting that corporates can fund.
    
-   **Business model:** corporates sponsor food banks based on measurable sustainability outcomes.
    
-   **Outcome:** more resources for food banks, better visibility into impact, and a clearer reason for companies to contribute.
    

### Best framing for the meeting

-   This is **not just a donations marketplace**
    
-   It is a **measurement + funding layer**
    
-   The wedge is **sustainability reporting**
    
-   The social benefit is stronger food bank capacity and broader reach
    

### Main open questions they were circling

-   Why would companies pay for this instead of funding normal CSR or carbon programs?
    
-   Does funding go to individual food banks or a shared pool?
    
-   Does the model reward already-strong food banks and create bad competition?
    
-   What exact data does a food bank need to input to generate a credible impact report?
    

### Stronger pitch

-   Food banks already create climate value by preventing food waste, but that value is rarely measured.
    
-   If we make that impact visible and easy to report, corporates can fund food banks through a channel that delivers both social and sustainability outcomes.
    
-   That gives food banks new resources without asking them to become sustainability experts.
    

### Biggest risk

-   If the marketplace logic dominates, it may push food banks to compete for sponsor attention instead of improving coverage where need is highest.

### Smart angle to add

-   Suggest a **blended allocation model**:
    
    -   some funding tied to measured impact
        
    -   some funding distributed by need
        
-   That keeps the sustainability incentive without creating a pure winner-takes-most dynamic
    

### One-line version

-   A platform that helps food banks measure avoided emissions from rescued food and turns that data into corporate funding.

### Two useful questions to ask

-   How do we make the corporate incentive strong enough that this is better than a normal sponsorship program?
    
-   How do we design the funding flow so it strengthens the whole food bank network, not just the best-reported food banks?
