# Tone of Voice — Climate-Action Packages

**One audience.** The Whale × Anthropic hackathon jury and the actual target buyer are essentially the same persona — fluent in CSRD vocabulary, allergic to marketing language, deciding in seconds. Write for both as one.

**Imagined target reader.** A Big-4 sustainability auditor reading skeptically. If your copy survives that, it survives Adriaan Mol, Duco van Lanschot, an ESG director at Heineken, and the auditor checking the disclosure six months later.

## Register

**Audit-grade, expert-level, UK English.** Not consumer, not academic — the register of a CSRD assurance memo or a sober ESG manager's internal brief. Restrained, precise, defensible.

## The seven rules

### 1. First sentence is the answer

Don't preface, don't soften. The reader gives you one sentence. Spend it on the answer, not on framing.

> ✓ *"€25,000 buys 600 tCO₂e of avoided emissions, attributed under the FRAME framework."*
> ❌ *"We believe that climate impact should be verifiable. That's why we built…"*

### 2. Numbers lead

Every claim earns a number. Big numbers go in tabular-nums at 36–72 px. Tooltips and tables show derivation.

> ✓ *"Voedselbank Rotterdam rescues 80,000 kg of food per week."*
> ❌ *"Voedselbank Rotterdam plays a major role in food rescue across the region."*

### 3. Cite or don't claim

Inline source labels, not footnotes. If a stat isn't traceable to a published source or a deterministic derivation, leave it out. Source labels go in muted text right after the stat — never a numbered footnote that requires a click.

### 4. Word-level discipline

**Banned vocabulary:** *passionate, mission-driven, leverage, unlock, empower, revolutionary, game-changing, our journey, cutting-edge, AI-powered, transform, supercharge, reimagine, disrupt, hero, champion, do well by doing good.*

**Use instead:** *deliver, document, attribute, verify, derived from, aligned with, in scope, compose, route, cap.*

### 5. Acronym hygiene

First use: *Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD)*.
Subsequent: *CSRD*. Same for *ESRS E1, ESRS S3, FRAME, ANBI, RDC, VBN*.

### 6. Active voice

It tells the reader who is responsible for what.

> ✓ *"Claude composes the report. Solvimon takes the payment."*
> ❌ *"Reports are generated by AI. Payments are processed."*

### 7. One paragraph per idea

Two paragraphs means you haven't decided which sentence is the point.

## Section-by-section calibration

| Surface | Register | Tip |
|---|---|---|
| Hero / headline | Sober declarative | One sentence, verb-first. *"Audit-ready climate impact through Dutch foodbanks."* |
| Marketplace cards | Dense product copy | Bank name → headline number → 2 stats → action. No adjectives. |
| Bank detail | Operator-fluent fact-led | One paragraph per section. End with the citation, not a flourish. |
| Coverage map | Same | Frame the data, don't editorialise. *"Top 3 NL gemeenten by poverty are covered. Heerlen is the next onboarding target."* |
| Impact data story | Slightly more narrative | Each section earns its place with a stat. The last sentence reframes — *"For context: VBN's 2024 budget was €4.2M."* |
| Methodology | Near-academic | This is the page that proves you're real. Cite everything. UK English most rigorously here. |
| Pricing (when built) | Conservative B2B sales | Tier names should be functional (Starter, Standard, Enterprise) not aspirational (Hero, Champion). |
| Customers (when built) | Restrained social proof | Logos with the qualifier "Pilot partners — pre-launch" so it's never deceptive. Pull quotes specific, not generic. |
| For-Foodbanks (when built) | Plain, warm, NL-friendly | This audience is volunteer operators, not ESG managers. Slightly softer. Still no marketing language. Bilingual NL/EN preferred. |
| Footer / 404s | Restrained | Even the empty states should sound like the rest of the site. |

## Three before/afters

❌ *"Our innovative platform leverages cutting-edge AI to revolutionise how corporates report their climate impact."*
✓ *"Climate-Action Packages translate Dutch foodbank operations into ESRS E1 disclosures. €25,000 = 600 tCO₂e attributed."*

❌ *"We're passionate about making a real difference for foodbanks and the planet."*
✓ *"Funds route to foodbanks under their existing ANBI structure. The platform retains a 5% fee."*

❌ *"Drive your CSR strategy forward with verifiable, AI-powered climate intelligence."*
✓ *"The quarterly report is ESRS-formatted. Drop it directly into your CSRD disclosure or forward to your auditor."*

## UK English checklist

| Use | Not |
|---|---|
| organisation | organization |
| centre | center |
| behaviour | behavior |
| favour | favor |
| categorise | categorize |
| per cent (in prose) | percent |
| 7.77% (in tables) | 7.77 percent |
| programme | program (unless software) |
| —, em-dash with hair-spaces | -- |
| €25,000 (€ before, comma 1000s) | $25,000 / EUR 25.000 |

## When in doubt

Read your sentence aloud. If it sounds like something a corporate would put in their LinkedIn caption, rewrite. If it sounds like something a sustainability analyst would put in a footnote of an ESRS statement, ship.

## Why this register wins Launch Ready

The jury reads commercial copy all day. They notice the *absence* of common marketing tells faster than they notice the presence of clever ones. A page that reads like a sober sustainability statement, with numbers that cite their sources, signals "this team understands the buyer" more than any clever turn of phrase.

The same restraint that wins the jury also lets a corporate ESG manager forward your page to their auditor without embarrassment. Operator-grade copy is the cheapest way to look credible.
