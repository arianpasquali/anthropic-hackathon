# Original Problem Spaces — Underexplored Populations

8 concrete problems not covered in standard research. Each targets a specific population with a specific situation.

---

## 1. Labour Migrants — Wage Theft and Unknown Rights ⭐ SELECTED

**Population:** 700,000+ Polish, Romanian, Bulgarian workers in NL (logistics, horticulture, cleaning, construction)
**Concrete situation:** Piotr works at a Schiphol warehouse. Employer deducts €600/month for a shared bunk bed from his €1,800 salary. He doesn't know this is illegal and actionable.
**Core rights violations:** Wage deductions below minimum wage (illegal), tied housing contracts (illegal), no ziekengeld when sick, no WW entitlement explained, no vakantiegeld paid
**Why it's missed:** Workers are mobile, often seasonal, speak no Dutch, afraid of deportation, invisible to mainstream NGOs
**AI angle:** Rights chatbot in Polish/Romanian/Bulgarian — "is this legal?" for any work/housing situation

---

## 2. Prison Reintegration — The 72-Hour Cliff

**Population:** 35,000 people released from Dutch prisons annually
**Concrete situation:** Released Monday 8am with €50 and a garbage bag. Bank account closed. Benefits need new aanvraag. ID expired. Housing requires income. Income requires housing. No smartphone, no DigiD, no address.
**The problem:** Each bureaucratic step requires the previous one. The system is circular with no entry point.
**Why it's missed:** Stigmatised population, "self-inflicted" perception, no political constituency
**AI angle:** Sequential step navigator that breaks circular dependency — what to do first, second, third, and why

## 3. Youth Aging Out of Jeugdzorg — The 18th Birthday Cliff

**Population:** ~400 Amsterdam youth/year losing care placement at 18
**Concrete situation:** Demi turns 18 in 3 months. Foster family contract ends on her birthday. Never applied for anything. No adult to ask.
**What they need simultaneously:** bijstand aanvraag, health insurance, bank account, housing — each requires the others
**Stats:** 40% homeless within 2 years of leaving care
**AI angle:** Countdown planner — what to arrange in the 3 months before turning 18, step by step

## 4. Mantelzorgers — Invisible Informal Caregivers

**Population:** 4.5M unpaid carers in NL; significant share immigrant women caring for elderly parents
**Concrete situation:** Fatima, 52, has cared for her mother with dementia 3 years, works part-time, losing income. Doesn't know she qualifies for respijtzorg, WMO-ondersteuning, mantelzorgcompliment (€300/yr), or legal right to reduce working hours.
**Why it's missed:** Caregiving is invisible, seen as family duty, no formal relationship with government
**AI angle:** Eligibility check for caregiver-specific benefits; rights explainer when employer refuses hours reduction

---

## 5. First-Generation Gastarbeiders with Dementia

**Population:** Turkish/Moroccan men from 1960s–70s migration wave, now 70–85, developing dementia
**Concrete situation:** Ali's father has Alzheimer's and only speaks Turkish. Dutch nursing home staff can't communicate. Ali's sister quit her job — no alternative.
**The gap:** Dutch dementia care is entirely Dutch-language. No culturally competent care exists at scale.
**AI angle:** Care coordination assistant for family members; translation layer for care decisions; rights explainer for WLZ indicaties

---

## 6. Passend Onderwijs — Non-Dutch Parents Fighting for Special Needs

**Population:** Non-Dutch-speaking parents of children with autism/ADHD/dyslexia in NL schools
**Concrete situation:** Sofia's son is autistic. School says he needs a TLV for special education. She doesn't speak Dutch, doesn't know which samenwerkingsverband to contact, what documents to prepare, or what rights she has if refused.
**The process:** TLV application → samenwerkingsverband → school board → WMO overlap — defeats most Dutch parents, near-impossible for non-Dutch speakers
**AI angle:** Step-by-step TLV navigator in multiple languages; draft bezwaar if refused

---

## 7. IND Procedure Opacity — Asylum Seekers in Bureaucratic Limbo

**Population:** People waiting 2–4 years for asylum decisions, living in AZC
**Concrete situation:** Mohamed has waited 2 years. Received an IND brief. Doesn't know if it's a rejection, a document request, or a positive decision — and has 4 weeks to respond if action is needed.
**Stakes:** Missing a response deadline can restart procedure or trigger deportation
**AI angle:** Letter reader for IND correspondence; procedure status explainer; deadline detector

---

## 8. People Leaving Closed Communities

**Population:** Ex-ultra-orthodox Jews, ex-Jehovah's Witnesses, people leaving highly conservative communities in Amsterdam
**Concrete situation:** Chana, 24, just left her ultra-orthodox community. No bank account, no secular work history, no knowledge of bijstand, no ID outside community documentation, social network severed overnight.
**Why it's missed:** Small population, high stigma, communities themselves are invisible to outsiders
**AI angle:** "Start from zero" guide — first 30 days checklist for someone who has never interacted with the Dutch state
