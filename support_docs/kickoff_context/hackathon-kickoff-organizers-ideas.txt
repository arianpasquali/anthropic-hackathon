some ideas based on the kickoff

-   **Solve a real Warchild problem, not a demo problem.** The kickoff says the team will present **actual challenges** they face, with the goal of solving something practical over the weekend. That suggests ideas should be concrete, narrow, and deployable.
    
-   **Bias toward real-world communication use cases.** One clear theme is **children’s communication systems**, which is probably the strongest domain signal in the notes.
    
-   **Build for current limits, not frontier ambition.** The notes emphasize that timelines are unclear, many people use AI too early, and the focus should be on **product usage** and practical limits rather than pure capability. That points toward simple, reliable workflows over ambitious agentic systems.
    
-   **Startup-style wedge thinking fits.** The “support across all stages” and “symbiotic relationship with startups” framing suggests judges may respond well to a project with a clear initial user, painful workflow, and believable expansion path.
