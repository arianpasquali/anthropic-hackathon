# Finalist Pitch Plan — Sunday 13:30–15:00

**If selected as one of the 10 finalists** (announced by 13:00 Sunday), the team gets:
- **2 min live demo on stage** — real laptop, real keyboard, no edits
- **2 min Q&A** — where Launch Ready is decided

Different game from the 1-min submission video. Slower visual pace, audience eye-contact, jury can interrupt. The Q&A is where 🚀 Launch Ready is won or lost.

## The 2-minute live demo

### Roles (lock these before walking on stage)
- **One driver** on the laptop — never speaks during the demo
- **One narrator** at the mic — never touches the laptop
- This avoids the classic two-people-fumble-for-the-keyboard hackathon disaster

### Beat sheet

| t | Beat | Driver does | Narrator says |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0:00–0:15** | Frame + ask | Marketplace home page, paused | *"Two minutes. We'll show this live. The full audit-ready CSR report appears at the end — please watch for it."* |
| **0:15–0:30** | Problem in 15s | Stays on home page | *"Every Dutch corporate now files CSRD climate disclosures. Most of what they claim is unverifiable. Meanwhile, Dutch foodbanks rescue 40 million kilos a year — and none of it counts."* |

Then scroll past Executive Summary → ESRS E1 table → Methodology Appendix. | *"Generated by Claude. Every figure traceable. ESRS E1 sections drop directly into the corporate's CSRD statement."* |

### Critical demo discipline

The single biggest risk is "demo doesn't load" at 0:55 with the jury watching. Mitigations, in order of priority:

1. **Pre-warm the dev server** before walking on stage; leave a Chrome tab open at the marketplace home
2. **Pre-render the report** so the Claude call is already cached in `out/`
3. **Have a backup laptop** running the same flow, ready to swap if the primary freezes
4. **Have screenshots of the report** as a final fallback — if the live render fails, the narrator says "and the audit-ready report looks like this" while the driver opens the screenshot
5. **Rehearse the 0:55 click path 20 times** — Solvimon → confirmation → report. Muscle memory.

### Q&A tactics

- **First sentence is the answer.** Don't preface. "No, and here's why" beats "That's a great question, what we've thought about is…"
- **Names matter, sparingly.** "Adriaan, you'd recognise this from Mollie's early days…" — once, only if it lands naturally
- **One number per answer.** More than one and the jury stops listening
- **Hand-off rule.** Pre-assign domains:
  - Technical Q → the FRAME / Claude builder
  - Business Q → the founder / pitch lead
  - Design Q → the UX person
  - Methodology Q → whoever wrote `factors.py` and can cite FAO/WRAP from memory
- **"I don't know" is acceptable.** "Honest answer: we haven't tested that scenario. Here's how we'd find out." beats inventing.
